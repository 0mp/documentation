\subsection*{SUB: subtract the value in r2 from that in r1}

\subsubsection*{Format}

\textrm{SUB \%rd, \%r1, \%r2}

\begin{center}
\begin{bytefield}[endianness=big,bitformatting=\scriptsize]{32}
\bitheader{0,7,8,15,16,23,24,31} \\
\bitbox{8}{0x06}
\bitbox{8}{r1}
\bitbox{8}{r2}
\bitbox{8}{rd}
\end{bytefield}
\end{center}

\subsubsection*{Description}

The \instruction{sub} instruction takes the value in \registerop{r2}
and subtracts it from that in \registerop{r1} placing the result in
\registerop{rd}. 

\subsubsection*{Pseudocode}

\begin{verbatim}
%rd = %r1 - %r2
\end{verbatim}

\subsubsection*{Load-time constraints}
The registers \registerop{r1}, \registerop{r2} and \registerop{rd} must be
valid registers and \registerop{rd} must not be \registerop{r0}.

\subsubsection*{Failure modes}

This instruction has no run-time failure modes beyond its constraints.
